Retro Stuff

When I recall the web of the 90s and early 2000s, I don't get nostalgic, but I am overwhelmed by the tragedy of missed opportunity. Enjoy this collection of links, tools and things from that era that could have, should have been, and hopefully still become.

Built With

The design of this website itself is a tribute to websites of that period.

Most of the animated GIFs on this site are from Internet Archive's GIFcities, a searchable repository of animated gifs from old Geocities websites.

Every single page is hand-coded and hand-linked, right down to the (if you find errors, please let me know). It's hosted on Neocities, a modern-day Geocities-like web host where you can find and follow lots of other old-school websites.

Tools and Utilities

Want to experience the the 90s and early 2000s first-hand? Here are some tools and utilies to get you started:

  • Webamp, lets you run Winamp 2 on your browser (and it works!)
  • Re:amp, a native clone of Winamp 2 for Mac OS X, with support for skins.
  • Floodgap Public Gopher Proxy, lets you access the old Gopher network (that precedes the web) through your web browser.
  • Castor, a graphical client for plain-text protocols written in Rust with GTK. It currently supports the Gemini, Gopher and Finger protocols.
  • DOS Games Archives, where you can browse, download and play over a thousand MS-DOS games like Doom, Bio Menace, Mario and AirTrax.
  • Gifsicle, a tiny command-line tool for *nix, Mac, Windows or even DOS to create, edit and optimise well-behaved animated gifs.
